Step 01

The Box Is Your Battlefield

Every night while you're sleeping, big money sets a range. That's your Asian Range box — the green and red zone on your chart.

Think of it like a fence. The top of the fence is where sellers live. The bottom is where buyers live. The dashed line in the middle? That's no man's land.

The Simple Version

Banks set the range overnight. NY comes in and picks a side. Your job is to figure out which side — before they do it.

Step 02

The Gold Line Is Everything

That yellow line labeled "Entry Line" with the price next to it? That's your 50% level. It's the midpoint of where institutions moved price from.

When price comes back to this line and reacts — that's your entry. Not before. Not after. On the reaction.

How To Use It

Wait for price to touch or get close to the gold Entry Line. Watch what the candles do there. If they reject it? That's your trade. If they blow through it? The bias might be flipping.

Step 03

M's Are Sells. W's Are Buys.

The indicator looks for double tops (M shapes) and double bottoms (W shapes) on your chart. But not just any M or W — it checks if the momentum underneath is actually weakening. That's called divergence.

Here's the cheat code:

M Above Asian Range = Sell

If you see an ◈ M ▼ label forming ABOVE the Asian Range box — that's a high-confluence sell. Banks swept the overnight high, made a double top, and momentum is dying. They're about to dump it.

W Below Asian Range = Buy

If you see a ◈ W ▲ label forming BELOW the Asian Range box — that's a high-confluence buy. Banks swept the overnight low, made a double bottom, and momentum is building. They're loading up.

The ◈ symbol means it formed outside the Asian Range — that's the A+ setup. A regular M or W without the ◈ is still valid, just lower conviction.

Step 04

Extreme Zones = Sniper Entries

Below your Entry Line, you'll see two tagged levels: Extreme Buys Lvl 1 and Extreme Buys Lvl 2. Above it, Extreme Sells Lvl 1 and Lvl 2.

These are the deep institutional levels where smart money actually fills orders. Lvl 1 is the first discount zone. Lvl 2 is the deep discount — the "they're never going to give us this price again" level.

The Key

Don't just blindly buy at extreme levels. Look for a W forming at or near an extreme buy level, or an M forming at or near an extreme sell level. When the pattern lines up with the zone — that's the sniper entry. That's confluence.

Step 05

The Bias Tells You Who's Winning

Before the NY session opens, the indicator reads how price behaved relative to the Entry Line. If most candles closed above it — ▲ STRONG BUYS. Below it — ▼ STRONG SELLS.

That's your starting bias. But it's not set in stone.

Live Bias Flip

If price prints 3 consecutive closes on the opposite side of the Entry Line during your session, the bias flips live. You'll see "◈ BIAS FLIPPED ▼ SELLS" — that means the market changed its mind. Respect it.

Step 06

The Box Changes Color For A Reason

When price fully closes outside the Asian Range box, the broken side changes color. If it breaks above — the top half turns green. Below — the bottom half turns red.

This tells you: which side got swept. If the top turned green and price is holding above the range — buyers are in control. If it breaks back inside, the colors reset. The market is still deciding.

Step 07

The A+ Trade

Here's what a perfect BLACK BOOK setup looks like:

🌙
Overnight: Asian Range builds. You see the box form with the dashed midline.
📊
Pre-session: Bias reads ▲ STRONG BUYS — most candles closed above the Entry Line heading into NY.
🎯
NY opens: Price dips below the Asian Range, hits your Extreme Buys Lvl 1, and prints a ◈ W ▲ divergence pattern.
💰
Entry: Price bounces back to the gold Entry Line. You enter on the reaction. SL below Extreme Buys Lvl 2. TP at Asian Range high or above.
That's The Playbook

Asian Range sets the field. Entry Line is your trigger. Extreme zones are your sniper spots. M's and W's confirm the move. Bias tells you the direction. Everything else is noise.
